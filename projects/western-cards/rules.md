### 1. Components

- **Card deck** – Any number of cards.
    - Each card shows **one die value** (1–6) and **one effect** (Switch / Peek / Shoot).
- **1 standard six-sided die (d6) per player**
- **Timer** (able to track 30 s and 5 s)
- **Discard pile** for used cards

***

### 2. Goal

Be the last player with at least **one face-down card** in front of you. If all your cards have been revealed or removed, you’re out.

***

### 3. Setup

1. Shuffle the deck and place it in the centre.
2. Randomly choose a starting player.
3. **Draft four face-down cards each**
    - Starting player draws ***player count + 1*** cards, secretly selects one, then passes the remainder clockwise.
    - Each subsequent player secretly takes one card and passes the rest.
    - The unchosen last card is discarded face-down.
    - Continue (new draw packets still = player count + 1) until every player has **exactly four face-down cards**.
4. Players may look at each of their four cards **once now**, then keep them face-down for the rest of the game.

***

### 4. Round Structure

A round has two phases:

PhaseSummary

**A. Shootout (3 shots each)**

Reveal, steal, and resolve effects.

**B. Re-draft (1 card each)**

Players with the fewest face-down cards start a quick one-card draft, then immediately start the next round.

#### Phase A – Shootout

1. **Table talk** – All players roll one d6 *simultaneously* and have **30 seconds** to discuss, bluff, and threaten (timer enforced).
2. **Three shot cycles** – Resolve three complete cycles in this order:
    1. **Determine shooter order**: player(s) with the *fewest* face-down cards shoot first. Ties are broken by the most recent player to place a card in front of them. The order is fixed for the whole round.
    2. **Each shooter’s turn** (repeat until everyone has fired once):
        1. **5 s** to point at an **opponent’s face-down card** (never your own).
        2. **Hit?** – If the revealed card shows the shooter’s die value:
            - Reveal it.
            - **Resolve its effect** (owner executes it).
            - Move it face-up to the shooter’s **trophy pile**.
            - **Trophy bonus** – The moment a player’s trophy pile reaches **four cards**, discard those three and draw **one face-down card** from the deck into their tableau.
        3. **Miss?** – Card stays face-down; nothing happens.
    3. Continue until every player has finished three shots (retaliatory shots do **not** count toward this limit).

##### Retaliatory **Shoot** effect

- Immediately after the hit is resolved, the card’s original owner:
    1. Rolls a d6.
    2. Must target one of the shooter’s face-down cards.
    3. Resolves that shot exactly as above.
- Retaliatory shots can chain if they reveal further **Shoot** cards.
- Retaliatory shots are **free** (do not spend one of your three normal shots).

##### Other effects

EffectResolution (owner performs)

**Switch**

Choose **two face-down** cards—one of yours, one of any opponent’s—and swap their positions (cards remain face-down).

**Peek**

Secretly look at any one opponent’s face-down card.

> **Memory rule:** After setup, you may only re-peek at one of your own cards **when it is being targeted**, to confirm its value.

#### Phase B – Re-draft

1. Identify the player(s) with the fewest face-down cards. If several tie, use the same tie-breaker as above.
2. The chosen player draws ***player left in game count + 1*** cards, keeps one face-down, and passes the rest clockwise.
3. Each other player keeps one card; the final unclaimed card is discarded face-down. All new cards enter face-down.
4. Start the next round with Phase A.

***

### 5. Elimination

- The moment a player has **zero face-down cards**, they are eliminated.
- Eliminated players **do take** take 1 shot before all other players shoot.

***

### 6. End of Game

Play rounds until only one player still possesses at least one face-down card. That player wins **Shootout Six**!
