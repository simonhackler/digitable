# Wasteland Run

Preliminary prototype for a card-only post-apocalyptic vehicle-building race.

## Target

2-4 players, 20-30 minutes, light tactical card game.

## Components

- Starter Vehicles deck: each player chooses or is dealt one chassis.
- Market Deck: vehicle parts, attacks, armor, engines, and crew.
- Road Deck: route cards that become each player's distance pile.
- Scrap can be tracked with face-down unused cards or any spare card marker.
- Damage can be tracked by rotating the vehicle card or placing damage cards under it.

## Setup

1. Each player takes one starter vehicle and sets hull, engine, and slots from that card.
2. Shuffle the Market Deck and reveal 5 cards in a shared market.
3. Shuffle the Road Deck.
4. Each player starts with 3 scrap.
5. The first player to collect 20 miles in their distance pile reaches the finish line and wins.

## Turn

On your turn, do these steps in order:

1. Gain 2 scrap.
2. Buy up to 1 card from the market by paying its cost, then refill the market to 5 cards.
3. Install any bought part if your vehicle has an open matching slot.
4. Drive by drawing road cards up to your engine value. Keep one as miles and resolve its hazard. Discard the rest.
5. Attack one rival if you have attack value. Deal damage equal to your attack minus their armor, minimum 0.

## Damage

When your vehicle has damage equal to or greater than its hull, discard one installed part and clear 2 damage. If you have no installed parts, lose 2 miles instead.

## Card Notes

- Engine increases how many road cards you can look at when driving.
- Speed adds bonus miles when a kept road card has at least 2 miles.
- Attack lets you damage rivals after driving.
- Armor reduces incoming attack damage and some hazards.
- Slot values are Weapon, Engine, Armor, Utility, or Crew.

## First Test Questions

- Is racing faster than fighting, or does attacking matter enough?
- Does the 5-card market give enough control?
- Is 20 miles the right finish distance?
- Are slots too restrictive for a first prototype?
